My Internship at the University of Bremen

A reflection on my work, learning, and experience in IT Service & Support

Some of the written content in this blog was generated with AI.

The Company

I completed my internship at the University of Bremen in the IT Service and Support department. This department is responsible for setting up and maintaining computer applications used in teaching and research. They repair PCs, manage networks, and install essential software across the university.

Workplace and Tools

IT specialists for system integration ensure that IT systems run reliably. They set up devices and networks, fix technical issues, and support users with IT-related questions. The training for this profession lasts three years and combines technical knowledge with practical teamwork.

Their main tasks include planning and configuring IT systems, operating networks, analyzing and fixing malfunctions, advising users, creating system documentation, and conducting small IT training sessions.

My Tasks During the Internship

During my internship, I disassembled a computer completely and rebuilt it from scratch. I installed different operating systems, including Windows and Linux, and developed two websites: a client‑side chat application and a task‑logging tool that later evolved into LogHue. At the end of my internship, I also gave a presentation about the components inside a computer.

My Experiences and Impressions

I honestly did not expect to learn so much during this internship. Even though I already knew the basics, the network simulation exercises helped me understand everything much better. One of the most important things I took away from this experience was the idea for the website I developed, which I have now expanded into something bigger that is still in development.

Overall, this opportunity gave me something truly valuable, and I am grateful to my teachers and my internship supervisor for their support.
